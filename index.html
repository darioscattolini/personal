About me

Bio

I'm Darío Scattolini, I'm 30 and was born in a small town in the province of Córdoba, Argentina. When I was 16 I moved to Córdoba city, where I got a degree in philosophy and started a PhD in history.

In 2018 my partner and I decided to move to Europe together. By then the idea of a career change was already maturing in my mind: I didn't find in teaching and research in the humanities the independence, security and work-life balance I was seeking. In 2019 we moved to Barcelona and I started learning web development.

My approach to coding

I quickly fell in love with programming, being the first time in years I dedicate so much time and enthusiasm to something. I love logic and maths since I was a child, so I feel very at ease with the kind of problem solving involved in coding. Barcelona's tech community also offered me the most welcoming environment to become a developer: besides studying on my own, I'm attending the IT Academy at Cibernàrium, and taking part in Codebar's programming workshops.

In the first months I gained a deeper understanding on how the web works and was already comfortable with HTML and CSS. Since then I'm following my Codebar mentors' advice of avoiding distraction by the overwhelming amount of technologies I can learn and delve deep only into JavaScript. Along the way I'm getting familiar with the rest of useful stuff (more advanced CSS features, Bootstrap, git, UX principles, etc.).

This advice helped me set a clearer goal: to master JavaScript and take advantage of its power, versatility and ubiquitousness. I'm focusing then on the JavaScript technology stack, getting quite confident with vanilla, starting to learn one of the front-end frameworks and expecting to reach the back-end with Node.js in the not so distant future.

Other things that interest me

I can't say all of these are regular habits, but I also love travelling, biking, tasting food and reading. I became particularly fond of American authors such as Carson McCullers, William Faulkner and Truman Capote (Southern Gothic literature, as I recently discovered).

However, my truly great passion is music. Music is the type of art and expression with which I reach the deepest connection, and at crucial moments of my life has helped me discover who I was and who I wanted to be. Today I'm devoting all my musical interest to techno , a genre on which I wish to bring to bear my coding skills, given the increasing use of computers in electronic music production and performance.
